PROBLEM STATMENT

A family system helps every one of its members through the good and the bad times. People want to contact their families more frequently, but so often find themselves distracted by work and their busy schedules, ultimately detaching from their loved ones.

Background

PROJECT OVERVIEW

During my UX/UI Bootcamp at IronHack, I was proposed with a project to The identify an opportunity (through user research) for an app to support building, or breaking, a habit. Furthermore, to craft a new mobile app in only mid-fi prototype, designed to meet the needs of the users based on research.

HYPOTHESIS

A smart tool that curates and suggests friendly reminders to contact people based on input will not only make contacting loved ones more attainable for busy users, but also encourage them to do so.

Research

INTERVIEWS

Due to time constraints and scope of the challenge, I conducted 5 face-to-face interviews with people who use their phones for more than 7 hours a day. All participants are aged between 27-36, living in Barcelona.

Can you tell me about how you use your phone?

I began all interviews with the question above. From there I interviewed each participant for around ~15 minutes to get down to their habits; thoughts; pain-points; even goals.

Interviewing them helped me better learn first-hand what habits are there to support or cut on and ultimately how they feel about it. I gained insight to how they have difficulties balancing their relationships with their families and work; their feelings and emotions when it comes to reaching out to family; or even their need to be constantly reminded.

Testing and Iterating

For the usability testing phase, I conducted nine thorough tests of the flow and the test results were amazing. I completed multiple iterations for each of the flows, as well as added a few pages to make the app complete. Although there were plenty of minor changes, I would like to shed light to the main and drastic changes of the design that were based off of the usability tests.

NAVIGATION BAR -> FLOATING BUTTONS

“It’s irrelevant”

“The home page is overwhelming”

“Minimalistic & Uncluttered”

“Ahhh much better”

Users were confused to where the sections of the Navigation bar at the bottom of the screen would take them. Some even said “It’s a little overwhelming for an app that should be simple and easy to use”. As a result, I decided to take a bold move and redesigned how users navigate around the app by using floating buttons on the top of the screen.

NAVIGATION BAR -> FLOATING BUTTONS

Initially, the ‘Connections’ page was very confusing to the users. The “Add” button was a little hidden. Furthermore, users felt like this page does not resonate with the feel and concept of the App. Hence, I redesigned the whole UI of it and made it feel and look more pleasant and easy to use. Making the ‘Add’ button very clear, as well as changed each contact card to be easily read and visualized.

NAVIGATION BAR -> FLOATING BUTTONS

“Are these buttons? Can I click on them?”

Initially, the reminders were only a checklist. Clicking on the radio button would mean that the task was complete. However through user testability, 9 users kept on clicking on the card, assuming it was a button. I then redesigned the reminders to be ‘click-able’ and giving the user the ability to choose between ‘Done’ or ‘Remind me later’.
