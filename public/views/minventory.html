Project Overview

The challenge of this project was to build a desktop web app/product to help professional teams get their work done more effectively.

Background

Problem Statement

Small businesses with limited budgets, spend time and money for material-inventory bookeeping yet end up losing clients and consistency. Hence, want to keep the business running and prevent interruptions.

Hypothesis

A B2B tool to track and manage professional inventory by integrating with POS systems to calculate when and how much inventory is needed to keep businesses running without interruptions.

Research

I chose to focus on small businesses, mainly because they were impacted the most by the pandemic and there isn’t a lot of SaaS tools targeted for them.

Interviews

I conducted 10 interviews with managers and/or owners of small to medium spas, dental clinics, and beauty centers in 7 different locations around the world.

Each interview took approximately 15-20 minutes and was initiated by asking them the following question:

“Could you walk me through an exhaustive management task at your business?”

Inisghts

Although the interviews were with business owners from different backgrounds and markets, I was fascinated that there were common patterns when it came to their pain-points.

Synthesis and Ideaditon

After gathering all the insights, I began to formulate the user personas, to be able to design the prototype with the user in mind.

User Personas

I came up with two user personas. What’s different is that they own different businesses, one is a beauty center and the other is a dental clinic. However, for the flow, I will focus on Brianna as her business is more complex and if we solve her problems, all other businesses’ problems will be solved.

Test, Iterate and Repeat

With the Mid-Fi ready, we began a usability testing sprint for three days with potential users. After conducting more than 20 usability tests we came across plenty of design faults and patterns that needed to be redesigned to meet user’s intuitions, familiarity, and wants. I decided to shed light to the 3 main iterations from the many that we had and will still do.

1.0 User Dashboard

17 users wanted to easily access posts that have been shared, as well as filter the content cards based on category.

”How can I filter the content to just see news or my company content?”

“Where can I see the content I’ve shared?”

We listened to our users and introduced sorting and filtering tools!

2.0 Scheduling Posts

12 users wanted the ability to schedule posts and or save as drafts, because in our initial design users were only able to share the post on-the-spot.

We added the ability to save as a draft and conducted more testing, and the responses we got were “its overwhelming” “too many decision making” . Therefore we decided to not do a ‘Save as Draft’ feature and rely on the favorites and scheduling features, where users will then have the ability to come back to it.

“What if I want to save a draft or schedule it to post tomorrow morning?”

“I’m hesitant about this post, can I save it as a draft?”

Ability to schedule posts and edit them using the schedule feature.

3.0 Content Cards

We initially designed the content cards with a huge emphasis and focus on hover. Throughout usability testing, users didn’t know how to ‘Favorite’ and ‘Share’. So we redesigned content cards to always present actions at all times. Furthermore, made alterations to the colors to align with the brand identity and be less overwhelming.
