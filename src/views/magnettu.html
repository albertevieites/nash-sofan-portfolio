First Thing's First

Project Scope

Magnettü contracted Michel, Jan and I to collaborate with their CTO to design and architect a MVP. They needed a company marketing landing page, user dashboard, and manager dashboard to show investors to realize the initial seed funding in Spring 2022.

Understanding the problem

Businesses that are struggling to lower cost per leads due to the loss of efficiency from social media channels, need a way to increase lead conversions and build brand awareness from within the business.

Hypothesis

Creating an easy tool to help employees play a more active in social media and managers to track content, will generate lead conversions at a lower cost. Hence, increase company sales, decrease marketing spending, and empower employees to build a personal brand.

Understanding The Audience

Although Magnettü’s team provided secondary research and a features list, we conducted further empathetic research to articulate a user-focused problem statement that will then allow us to craft a MVP design that meets the user’s needs.

Interviews

We began our primary research by creating an interview guide then started conducting 12 interviews with participants aged between 26-41 who are currently in the workforce and are potential users. Participants were a mix of brand managers and employees in multiple industries so we can understand the needs and wants from each use case. We then did affinity mapping and feature prioritization methods to help us ideate.

Interview Findings

Managers complain about social media ads expenses and want lucrative alternatives.

Of managers want employees to share company news because it cheaper than social media ads and are percieved as trustworthy.

Of employees would love to share content, but find it time consuming and difficult to come up with content.

Source: User Research Spring 2022

Test, Iterate and Repeat

With the Mid-Fi ready, we began a usability testing sprint for three days with potential users. After conducting more than 20 usability tests we came across plenty of design faults and patterns that needed to be redesigned to meet user’s intuitions, familiarity, and wants. I decided to shed light to the 3 main iterations from the many that we had and will still do.

1.0 User Dashboard

17 users wanted to easily access posts that have been shared, as well as filter the content cards based on category.

”How can I filter the content to just see news or my company content?”

“Where can I see the content I’ve shared?”

We listened to our users and introduced sorting and filtering tools!

2.0 Scheduling Posts

12 users wanted the ability to schedule posts and or save as drafts, because in our initial design users were only able to share the post on-the-spot.

We added the ability to save as a draft and conducted more testing, and the responses we got were “its overwhelming” “too many decision making” . Therefore we decided to not do a ‘Save as Draft’ feature and rely on the favorites and scheduling features, where users will then have the ability to come back to it.

“What if I want to save a draft or schedule it to post tomorrow morning?”

“I’m hesitant about this post, can I save it as a draft?”

Ability to schedule posts and edit them using the schedule feature.

3.0 Content Cards

We initially designed the content cards with a huge emphasis and focus on hover. Throughout usability testing, users didn’t know how to ‘Favorite’ and ‘Share’. So we redesigned content cards to always present actions at all times. Furthermore, made alterations to the colors to align with the brand identity and be less overwhelming.
